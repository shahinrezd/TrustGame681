Introductions / Task 1

You have been randomly and anonymously paired with another participant. One of you will be selected at random to be participant A; the other will be participant B. You will learn whether you are participant A or B prior to making any decision.

To start, participant A receives {{ Constants.endowment }}; participant B receives nothing. Participant A can send some or all of their endowment to participant B. Before B receives this amount, it will be multiplied by {{ Constants.multiplier }}. Once informed of the amount sent to them, B can decide to offer some, or all of it to A. participant A must then accept or deny B's offer. you will perform this task for 5 rounds, and will be paired up randomly each time. If A accepts, A receives whatever B sent them, along with the remainder of whatever was not sent (if any). If A accepts, B receives whatever was sent to them minus what they sent to A. If the offer is rejected, A keeps the remainder, and B receives 0.

Suppose participant A sends 40 of their 100 points to B, leaving 60 as a remainder. This amount will then be tripled by the computer and B will receive 120 points. Suppose B offers 50 points back to A. If A accepts the offer, B will end with 70 points, and A will end with 110 points. If A rejects this offer, B will end with 0 points, while A will keep their remainder of 60 points. You will be asked to complete this task 5 times. You will be randomly paired each time. There is no guarantee that you will be paired with the same person again.

After the experiment is finished, you will asked to complete a short questionnaire. As a thank you for completing this experiment you will be put in a draw to randomly win a prize.
